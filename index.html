Screen-Free Foundations (Ages 0-5)

Theme: Protecting the Senses

Key Focus: Young children learn about God's world by touching, tasting, and physically moving through it. Screens skip this vital step.

The Challenge of Early Tech

At this stage, children are "Artists." Their brains are not ready to process the fast-paced, flashing lights of tablets and TVs. When we use screens to constantly quiet them, they miss learning how to self-soothe or handle boredom.

Action Steps for Parents

  • Turn off background TV: It disrupts their focus while playing.
  • Be the "Keeper": Keep the phone away during meals and bedtimes. When you meet their needs physically (not digitally), you teach them that God provides.
  • Swap Swiping for Sorting: Give them real-world tasks like stacking blocks or playing with water instead of tapping a screen.

Take-Home Idea: The Phone Basket

When you get home from work, put your phone in a basket by the door for the first 30 minutes. Let your child see your face before they see the back of your phone.

The Scientist & The Smartphone (Ages 6-10)

Theme: Engaging Interests while Guarding the Heart

Key Verse: "All things are lawful for me, but I will not be dominated by anything." (1 Corinthians 6:12)

The Science of the "Scroll"

Elementary kids love collecting facts and discovering how things work. Apps and games are designed to give their brains a constant loop of rewards. If a child throws a fit when the iPad is taken away, they aren't just being bad—their brain has been hijacked. Proverbs 25:28 says a person without self-control is like a city with broken walls. We have to build the walls for them.

Identifying the Symptoms

  • Irritability when away from a screen.
  • Losing interest in physical hobbies (sports, Legos, drawing).
  • If the device "dominates" their mood, it is becoming an idol.

The 3-to-1 Rule

For every hour of "Screen Discovery" (like playing Minecraft or watching YouTube), require three hours of "Real World Discovery" (playing outside, reading a physical book, family game night).

The Engineer & Digital Identity (Ages 11-13)

Theme: Affirming Identity in a Digital World

Key Verse: "Am I now seeking the approval of man, or of God?... If I were still trying to please man, I would not be a servant of Christ." (Galatians 1:10)

The Engineered Self

Middle schoolers are trying to figure out who they are. Social media gives them tools to "engineer" a fake, perfect version of themselves. When they stare at perfectly filtered photos of others, they start to hate their own real, "fearfully and wonderfully made" bodies.

From "Police" to "Partner"

  • If you only "police" their phone by snooping in secret, kids will just learn to hide things better.
  • If you "partner" with them, you talk openly about the hard things online.
  • Create a no-shame zone: Let them know they can come to you if they see something bad online without fear of losing their phone instantly.

Take-Home Action: The Non-Digital Night

Commit to one night a week where all phones are put away. Use this face-to-face time to tell them: "I love you for who you are, not for what you post. You don't have to be perfect to be loved in this house."

The Philosopher & Digital Discernment (Ages 14-18)

Theme: Mobilizing Wisdom in a Digital World

Key Verse: "Look carefully then how you walk, not as unwise but as wise, making the best use of the time." (Ephesians 5:15-16)

The Digital Public Square

For high schoolers, the internet is where they encounter every different belief and worldview. We do not want them to simply hide from the world; we want them to learn how to walk wisely within it. This is the stage where you must shift from a "Manager" who makes the rules to a "Consultant" who helps them set their own boundaries.

The Consultant Question Bank

When they are struggling with screen time, ask these questions instead of yelling:

  • "Does this app make it easier or harder for you to love God and focus?"
  • "How does your current phone use match up with the kind of adult you want to be?"
  • "How can I help you take back the time that the algorithm is stealing?"

Take-Home Challenge: Digital Missionaries

Instead of asking what they need to stop doing online, ask what they could start doing. Challenge them to use their social media or gaming accounts to show kindness, encourage others, and be a light for Christ.

Family Tech Covenant

Family Tech Covenant

"All things are lawful for me, but I will not be dominated by anything." - 1 Corinthians 6:12

We believe that technology is a tool given to us to create, connect, and learn. However, we also know it can be a distraction from what matters most: loving God and loving each other. We agree to the following commitments:

1. We will protect our time and rest.

We agree that sleep is a gift from God. Therefore, all personal screens will be turned off and plugged into the family charging station by: PM.

2. We will prioritize people over screens.

When we are eating meals together, riding in the car as a family, or someone is talking directly to us, we will put our devices down and give our full attention.

3. We will act with honesty and integrity.

We will not hide apps, use secret accounts, or delete browser history. Parents promise to review devices as mentors who want to help, not just as police looking for a crime.

4. We will ask for help when we mess up.

If we see something online that makes us uncomfortable, scared, or tempted, we promise to tell a parent or trusted adult immediately. Parents promise to stay calm and help, without immediately threatening to take the device away forever.
